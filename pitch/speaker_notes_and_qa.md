We built a practical capability to close this execution gap. It gives ministries a structured path from alert to triage to action, while embedding legal framing and preserving human decision authority.

This is not another dashboard. It is a governance-to-action system for AI-enabled disinformation response, designed for policy teams, communications teams, and operational leads working together under time pressure.

By the end of this briefing, we are asking for one clear decision: endorse a ministry-led 90-day adoption framework.

## Q&A Objection Handling Bank

### 1) How do we prevent false positives?
Suggested answer:
- We treat outputs as decision support, not autonomous verdicts.
- Human review remains mandatory for consequential actions.
- Risk bands and evidence fields standardize judgment rather than automate it blindly.

### 2) Is this politically neutral?
Suggested answer:
- The workflow is content-risk and harm focused, not ideology focused.
- Decision pathways emphasize verification, source-citation, and proportional response.
- Oversight controls and logs support accountability.

### 3) Will this increase legal exposure?
Suggested answer:
- The opposite: structured documentation reduces arbitrary handling and improves defensibility.
- The system makes legal context visible at point of action.
- Governance ownership and pilot controls further reduce deployment risk.

### 4) Is this replacing public servants?
Suggested answer:
- No. It augments public servants under time pressure.
- It reduces repetitive workflow friction and increases quality consistency.
- Final decisions and escalation ownership remain fully human.

### 5) Is this difficult to adopt across units?
Suggested answer:
- The rollout model is staged and practical.
- Start with one lead unit, then expand based on measured outcomes.
- Toolkit components are intentionally non-technical for mixed teams.

### 6) How do we prove value quickly?
Suggested answer:
- Use pilot KPIs from day one: alert-to-triage time, decision-to-action time, citation coverage, and recurrence rates.
- 90 days is sufficient to produce directional evidence for endorsement and scale decisions.
